I have found that there seems to be a small dichotomy between Quantum Physics and Quantum Information Processing, and although I have not had a very significant journey into the world of quantum physics, but that hasn't substantially handicapped me in terms of my ability to understand quantum information processing.

The mathematics used in quantum computation are fairly straight-forward it is really just simple linear algebra. The main difference in this and other types of linear algebra is the specific kiund of notation that is used. Although there is nothing particularly novel except the notation that is used, it was developed by the physicist Paul Dirac a good introduction is here here there is a little bit more condensed guide that does not contain some of the examples from the first document here.
